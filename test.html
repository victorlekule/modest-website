Visa, Insurance & Entry Requirements

Yes, most international passport holders require a tourist visa. You can easily get a Visa on Arrival at the airport or apply in advance via the official Tanzania eVisa online portal.
  • Cost: $50 USD for most nationalities ($100 USD strictly for US Citizens due to a bilateral agreement).
  • Requirement: Your passport must be valid for at least 6 months from your arrival date with at least 2 blank pages.
Yes, absolutely. As of official government regulations, all international visitors entering the island must purchase the official insurance managed by the Zanzibar Insurance Corporation (ZIC). It costs $44 USD per person and is valid for 92 days.

You must secure this QR-coded certificate online via the official portal (inbound.visitzanzibar.go.tz) before boarding your flight, even if you have global premium insurance.
🟡 Yellow Fever: A Yellow Fever vaccination certificate is strictly required only if you are arriving from or transiting for more than 12 hours through a country with a risk of Yellow Fever transmission (e.g., Kenya, Ethiopia). If you are flying directly from Europe or the US without a long African transit, it is not mandatory.

🦟 Malaria: Zanzibar and safari parks are malaria-risk zones. We highly advise consulting your travel doctor at home regarding recommended anti-malarial prophylactics and packing a high-DEET insect repellent.

Safari & Bush Logistics

We value your holiday time, so we bypass long overland road journeys. Morix Beyond uses Fly-In Safaris. We pick you up privately from your beach resort, take you to Zanzibar Airport (ZNZ), and place you on a light aircraft that flies directly into the safari park airstrips (Serengeti, Ngorongoro, or Mikumi) within 1.5 to 2 hours.
Light aircraft used for bush flights have strict weight and space limits for safety.
  • 🎒 The Rule: Maximum 15 kg (33 lbs) per person, packed strictly in soft-sided duffel bags or backpacks. Hard-shell suitcases with wheels cannot fit in the plane’s luggage pods.
  • 🧳 Morix Service: You can safely leave your heavy hard-shell bags at your Zanzibar hotel.

Zanzibar Island Life & Culture

Zanzibar is a predominantly Islamic island with rich cultural traditions. While bikinis and swimsuits are completely fine at hotel pools and beaches, you must dress modestly when entering public areas like Stone Town, local spice farms, and villages. Loose linen clothing is highly recommended for the tropical climate.
No. While luxury resorts and established companies like Morix Beyond accept secure online card payments, Zanzibar is still a cash-based economy. Local beach bars, souvenir shops, and tipping require cash.

💵 Pro Tip: Bring US Dollars (USD) printed after 2013. Older bills are strictly rejected by local banks and shops due to counterfeit regulations. ATMs are readily available in Stone Town but very rare in remote beach villages.
