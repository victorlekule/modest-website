Mandatory Inbound Travel Insurance for Zanzibar

All visitors traveling to Zanzibar are required to have mandatory inbound travel insurance issued by the Zanzibar authorities before arrival. This is separate from your personal travel insurance and is mandatory for entry.

  • Accidents & Evacuation

Best Time to Visit Zanzibar for Beach Lovers

Zanzibar is famous for its powder-white beaches, warm turquoise waters, and breathtaking sunsets. Whether you are planning a relaxing holiday, a romantic escape, or a photography trip, choosing the right time to visit can make a big difference in your experience.

Weather Overview

Zanzibar has a tropical climate with warm temperatures throughout the year, usually between 25°C and 32°C. The island experiences two main seasons: dry and rainy. The dry seasons are generally sunny, less humid, and ideal for outdoor activities. The rainy seasons bring short or heavy showers, higher humidity, and rougher sea conditions. Understanding these seasons will help you plan the perfect beach holiday.

Top Seasons for Beach Activities

June to October

Cooler temperatures, low humidity, clear oceans. Perfect for:

  • Watersports
  • Sandbank Excursions
  • Sunset Dhow Cruises
  • Swimming & Snorkeling
  • Drone & Pro Photography

December to February

Hotter, mostly dry and sunny. Warm seas. Perfect for:

  • Beach holidays
  • Escaping Winter
  • Honeymoon Trips
  • Diving & Watersports
  • Romantic Beach Dinners

Months to Avoid (Or Travel With Caution)

March to May (Long Rainy Season):This period is the main rainy season in Zanzibar. Heavy rain can last for hours or even days, and the ocean may be rough. During this time:

November (Short Rains): November has short and unpredictable rain showers. Travel is still possible, but conditions are less stable than during the dry months.

Best Time to Visit Tanzania for Safari

1

Dry Season (June – October)

This is the best time for safaris. Animals gather near water sources, and roads are easier to travel.

2

Short Dry Season (December – February)

Another excellent safari period with warm temperatures and baby animals being born in some parks.

3

Rainy Season (March – May)

This is the low season with heavy rain. Travel is cheaper, and parks are green, but animal spotting is harder.

Quick Tips

Visa & Entry

Most travelers can obtain a visa on arrival or online before traveling. Always check current visa rules before departure.

Currency

US dollars and Tanzanian shillings are widely accepted. Carry cash for local shopping.

Local Culture

Zanzibar is a Muslim-majority island. Dress modestly in towns and villages, especially outside beach areas.

Transport

Private transfers are the most comfortable and reliable way to move around the island.

Best Beaches in Zanzibar

  • Kendwa Beach Kendwa, northern Zanzibar – wide sandy beaches, breathtaking sunsets, beach bars, boat trips, and unforgettable vibes.
  • Paje Beach Paje, a top spot for kitesurfing and adventure seekers, offers strong winds, shallow waters, and a laid-back backpacker vibe.
  • Matemwe Beach Matemwe, a serene beach facing Mnemba Island, is perfect for snorkeling and travelers seeking privacy and natural beauty.
  • Jambiani Beach Jambiani – long sandy beaches, charming fishing villages, and the perfect spot for cultural vibes and relaxing strolls.
