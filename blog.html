Everything You Need to Know About the Mandatory Zanzibar Inbound Travel Insurance

Planning your dream escape to the white sands of Zanzibar? There is one critical step you cannot afford to miss before boarding your flight. As of recent official government mandates, all international visitors entering Zanzibar are strictly required to purchase the official Zanzibar Inbound Travel Insurance.

What is it?

It is a government-mandated local insurance policy managed strictly by the Zanzibar Insurance Corporation (ZIC). This policy covers international tourists for local medical emergencies, luggage losses, flight delays, and emergency evacuations while on the island.

"I already have global insurance."

Yes, it is still mandatory. Even if you hold a premium worldwide policy, the local Zanzibar government insurance is required by law. Immigration will check for the official ZIC QR code upon arrival.

Cost & Validity

💰 Approximately $44 USD per person. ⏱ Valid for up to 92 days from entry.

How & When to Buy

Purchase online before departure via the certified government portal. Do not wait until landing. You'll receive a QR code – print or save it on your phone. Only use the official ZIC portal to avoid scams.

What to Wear in Zanzibar: Respect Culture & Beat the Heat

Beach & Resort

Bikinis, swimsuits, and shorts are perfectly fine at your hotel pool, beaches, and during water sports like Safari Blue.

Stone Town & Villages

Dress modestly. Cover shoulders and knees. Loose linen trousers, maxi dresses, and short‑sleeved shirts are ideal.

Footwear

Leave high heels at home. Stone Town's coral‑stone alleys demand comfortable walking shoes or sandals.

Cash vs. Cards: Navigating Money, Tipping & ATMs in Zanzibar

While luxury resorts accept cards, Zanzibar is still a cash‑based economy. Here’s everything you need to know.

Which Currency?

Carry both USD and Tanzanian Shillings. Ensure USD bills are printed after 2013 – older ones are often rejected.

ATMs Are Limited

ATMs exist at the airport and Stone Town, but are scarce in remote beach areas. Withdraw enough cash before heading to Matemwe or Kizimkazi.

Tipping Culture

Not mandatory, but appreciated. For private drivers, boat captains, and safari guides, $10–$20 USD per day from the group is a generous standard.
